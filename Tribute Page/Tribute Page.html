Nelson Mandela

The first black president of South Africa

Here is a timeline of Nelson Mandela's Life

  • July 18, 1918: Born Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela in Mvezo, South Africa
  • 1948: Completed his law degree and joined the ANC.
  • 1944: Co-founded the ANC Youth League.
  • 1948: The National Party came to power, implementing apartheid policies.
  • 1952:Led the Defiance Campaign against apartheid and was banned from public life.
  • 1956: Arrested and charged with high treason along with 155 others.
  • 1960: The ANC was banned following the Sharpeville Massacre.
  • 1961: Co-founded the ANC's armed wing, Umkhonto we Sizwe (MK).

    • Imprisonment and release

  • 1962: Arrested and sentenced to five years in prison.
  • 1964: Sentenced to life imprisonment for sabotage at the Rivonia Trial.
  • February 11, 1990:Released from Victor Verster Prison after 27 years of imprisonment.

    • Post-Prison Life & Presidency

  • 1990s:Led negotiations with President F. W. de Klerk to dismantle apartheid.
  • 1993:Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize jointly with F. W. de Klerk.
  • April 27, 1994:South Africa's first democratic, multiracial general elections were held.
  • May 10, 1994: Inaugurated as the first democratically elected President of South Africa.
  • 1999:Stepped down after one term as president.
  • December 5, 2013: Died at the age of 95.

"Do not judge me by my successes, judge me by how many times I fell down and got back up again" --Nelson Mandela
