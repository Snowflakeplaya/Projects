Logarithmic Derivatives

A Professional Study Guide: Core Formulas and Worked Examples

1. Derivatives of the Natural Logarithm ($\ln x$)

The natural logarithm, $\ln x$, is the logarithm to the base $e$. Its derivative forms the foundational rule for all logarithmic functions. The core formula relies on the **Chain Rule**, where $u$ represents any differentiable function of $x$.

The General Rule for Natural Log:

$$ D_x[\ln u] = \frac{u'}{u} $$

*Where $u$ is the inside function and $u'$ is its derivative.*

Key Examples and Techniques

Example A: Polynomial Inside (Chain Rule)

Find $D_x[\ln(x^2 + 4)]$

Here, $u = x^2 + 4$ and $u' = 2x$.

$$ D_x[\ln(x^2 + 4)] = \frac{u'}{u} = \frac{2x}{x^2 + 4} $$

Example B: Using Log Properties ($\ln u^n = n \ln u$)

Find $D_x[\ln x^3]$.

Simplify first: $\ln x^3 = 3 \ln x$.

$$ D_x[3 \ln x] = 3 \cdot D_x[\ln x] = 3 \cdot \frac{1}{x} = \frac{3}{x} $$

Example C: Trigonometric Functions

Find $D_x[\ln(\sin x)]$

Here, $u = \sin x$ and $u' = \cos x$.

$$ D_x[\ln(\sin x)] = \frac{\cos x}{\sin x} = \cot x $$

2. Derivatives of General Logarithms ($\log_a x$)

When the base of the logarithm is any number $a$ (where $a > 0, a \neq 1$) instead of $e$, an extra term is introduced to the denominator. This term is simply $\ln a$.

The General Rule for Log Base $a$:

$$ D_x[\log_a u] = \frac{u'}{u \ln a} $$

*Note: This formula reduces to the Natural Log rule when $a = e$, since $\ln e = 1$.*

Worked Examples with Base $a$

Example D: Simple General Log

Find $D_x[\log_3 x]$

Here, $u = x$, $u' = 1$, and $a = 3$.

$$ D_x[\log_3 x] = \frac{1}{x \ln 3} $$

Example E: Log with Chain Rule

Find $D_x[\log_7 (5 - 2x)]$

Here, $u = 5 - 2x$, $u' = -2$, and $a = 7$.

$$ D_x[\log_7 (5 - 2x)] = \frac{-2}{(5 - 2x) \ln 7} $$

Example F: Log with Trigonometric Inside

Find $D_x[\log_5 (\tan x)]$

Here, $u = \tan x$, $u' = \sec^2 x$, and $a = 5$.

$$ D_x[\log_5 (\tan x)] = \frac{\sec^2 x}{(\tan x) \ln 5} $$
